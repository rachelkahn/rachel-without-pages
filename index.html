This demo will show

how one can hover over

an image to enlarge it!

Once you've linked your desired image, create a div and name it "image container." Give your image a size, such as 50px X 100px and then move onto your CSS. Within your styles, create a section .imagecontainer and start playing around with the hover effect! To create the effect, within your .image container, you will type in 'image:hover' which will signal the effect. To make the hover change your images size, you will then write the new size measurements, such as 500px X 800px. This will create an enlargment effect when the mouse hovers over the small image. Creating a moment for the reader to view the imagery without too many other elements taking precedence.
